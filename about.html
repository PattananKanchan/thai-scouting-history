About Thai Scouting

Key figures in the founding of Thai Scouting

King Rama VI (King Vajiravudh)

King Rama VI is the founder of Thai Scouting. On July 1, 1911, he officially introduced Scouting to Thailand, inspired by Lord Baden-Powell, the founder of the worldwide Scout Movement. His vision was to instill loyalty, discipline, and unity among Thai youth.

Another Important Royal Duties
    - Established radio and telegraph stations in Bangkok (Sala Daeng Subdistrict) and in Songkhla Province.
    - Established the Government Savings Bank in 1913.
    - Led Thailand into World War I by siding with the Allies, who were victorious.
    - Composed approximately 200 books, articles, poems, plays, and short stories in both prose and poetry.

Lord Baden-Powell

Lord Baden-Powell, the founder of the global Scout Movement in England, inspired the creation of Thai Scouting. His methods and ideals were adapted by King Rama VI to suit Thai culture and society.
