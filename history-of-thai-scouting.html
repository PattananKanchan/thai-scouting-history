History of Thai Scouting

A legacy of discipline, unity, and service since 1911

Foundation

Thai Scouting was established on July 1, 1911 by King Rama VI (King Vajiravudh). Inspired by Lord Baden-Powell, he founded Scouting to instill discipline, loyalty, and civic responsibility in young people.

Early Growth

The first Scout troops began in Bangkok schools and quickly spread across the nation. By the 1920s, Scouting had become an integral part of Thai education and youth development.

Role in Society

Thai Scouts supported communities during national ceremonies, natural disasters, and social projects. Their motto, “Be Prepared,” has guided generations of youth to serve their country faithfully.

Modern Scouting

Today, millions of Thai Scouts continue to embrace values of leadership, teamwork, and service. More than a century later, Thai Scouting remains a symbol of national pride.
